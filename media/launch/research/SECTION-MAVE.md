# Research Report: The Mave Health Case Study

Source: `subah-playbook.md`, section "Back in March we ran the launch for @mavehealth..." through "The takeaway".

## Summary

Mave Health launched a $500 brain-stimulation wearable on X — a platform with no healthtech launch playbook — and converted 2.5M+ views into six figures of pre-order revenue in one day, even surviving a 40-minute X outage mid-launch. The core lesson: they optimized every decision (copy, creators, timing, replies) for a single conversion metric rather than reach, led with proof instead of hype, layered tiered influencer amplification over a press anchor, and ran the launch in real time from a war room. For meme-maker, this maps directly: pick one metric (installs / GitHub stars), lead with concrete proof (609 templates, deterministic rendering, one-line curl install), line up a tiered amplification network of AI/devtool accounts before launch morning, and treat launch day as a live operation where every reply and quote-tweet is worked in real time.

## Tactics

- **Optimize for one conversion metric, not vanity views.** Every decision at Mave pointed at US pre-orders, not impressions. `For meme-maker:` pick the launch KPI up front — e.g. `curl` installs or GitHub stars in 48h — and shape copy, targeting, and CTAs around it (install command in the launch tweet, not just a demo).
- **Lead with proof in the first two lines.** Mave opened with "We've raised $2.1M to fix your focus" plus users at Google/UFC/YC — credibility before the pitch. `For meme-maker:` open with hard specifics ("609 templates, deterministic output, works from any agent via MCP — one curl to install") instead of adjectives; cut "revolutionary/game-changing" style words entirely.
- **Cut generic category language; speak the buyer's dialect.** Trust-first copy avoided wearable clichés. `For meme-maker:` write like an engineer to engineers — show the actual CLI/MCP invocation and output image in the launch post; the demo IS the copy.
- **Run amplifiers in tiers with distinct jobs.** ~70 creators quote-posted (credibility layer), hundreds of reposters carried reach, and small accounts hashtagged/tagged the founder to push onto Today's News. `For meme-maker:` recruit 3 tiers before launch: (1) known AI-Twitter/devtool accounts to quote-tweet with their own takes, (2) friendly builders to repost, (3) small accounts posting memes *made with meme-maker* tagging Kartik.
- **Tier the copy per audience.** Finance crowd got a different angle than the health crowd. `For meme-maker:` prep distinct QT angles: agent-builders ("give your agent a sense of humor via MCP"), CLI/devtools people ("deterministic image gen from the terminal"), indie hackers ("free, MIT, 609 templates"), meme accounts (just funny output).
- **Anchor the launch to press/content dropping first.** Dhawal's tweet went live ~2h after the TechCrunch story so credibility sat under the launch. `For meme-maker:` land an anchor artifact first — a Hacker News post, a blog write-up, or the launch video — and time the X thread a couple hours after so QTs have something substantial to point at.
- **Sequence the day: influencers carry hours 0–4, founder's network carries the afternoon.** The second wave catches fresh people as the first fades. `For meme-maker:` schedule tier-1 QTs within minutes of the launch tweet, then activate Kartik's personal network (DMs pre-sent, ready to reply/QT) for the afternoon second wave.
- **Run a war room and reply in real time.** A dedicated Slack channel, everyone awake, working every reply and quote as it landed — including through the X outage. `For meme-maker:` block launch day fully; reply to every comment fast, casually, with memes generated live by meme-maker itself (the replies double as product demos).
- **Keep replies human and a little funny, never PR speak.** Authenticity wins in the mentions. `For meme-maker:` this is a native advantage — reply with generated memes, self-deprecating jokes, and raw founder voice; zero corporate tone.
- **Momentum compounds in a few hours — engagement velocity decides algorithmic reach.** A million views by late morning kept the algorithm feeding it. `For meme-maker:` front-load everything into the first 2–4 hours (7–9am PT weekday); don't dribble the launch out over days.

## Don't do this (anti-patterns)

- Don't lead with hype adjectives ("revolutionary", "game-changing") — proof beats hooks.
- Don't optimize for impressions/likes; a viral post with no installs is a failed launch.
- Don't post and walk away — a launch unattended in its first four hours dies.
- Don't reply in corporate/PR voice; nobody wants a press release in their mentions.
- Don't send every audience the same copy; one-size-fits-all angles convert nobody.
- Don't launch without a second wave planned — the influencer push fades by afternoon.
- Don't panic-abandon the launch if something breaks (X outage, site down); adapt live from the war room.

## Concrete next steps for the meme-maker launch

1. Define the single launch KPI (e.g. installs or stars in 48h) and put the install command / repo link as the sole CTA in the launch post.
2. Draft trust-first launch copy: numbers and demo up top (609 templates, MCP/CLI/HTTP, MIT), no hype words; pair with the launch video.
3. Build the tiered amplifier list now: tier-1 AI/devtool QT accounts, tier-2 reposters, tier-3 small accounts posting memes made with meme-maker; pre-write per-audience QT angles.
4. Pick the anchor: submit to Hacker News (or publish a launch blog post) ~2h before the X thread goes live at 7am PT on a weekday.
5. Set up a launch-day war room (Slack/Discord channel), pre-draft reply memes, and commit to working every reply for the full day plus a second-wave push from Kartik's own network in the afternoon.
