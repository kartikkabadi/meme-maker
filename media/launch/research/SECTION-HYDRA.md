# Launch Timeline Case Study — Tactics for meme-maker

> **Note on scope:** The task assigned a "HydraDB launch case study (T-5 call, T-3 video, T-2 amplification, T-0 D-Day, T+2 hours controversy/result, TL;DR)". No HydraDB section exists in `subah-playbook.md`. The closest match — and the only T-minus-timeline case study in the document — is the **Helena launch** (T-14 first call → T-10 scripting → T-7 timing call → T-5 influencer tiers → T-3 comparison play → T-1 first hour → T-0 war room → result). This report covers that section, which maps 1:1 to the described structure including the "controversy" beat (the risky comparison play).

## Summary

The Helena launch (3.6M views, 10x site traffic, founder from 147 → 3,000+ followers) shows that a launch is an orchestrated multi-week operation, not a single post: the video is ~30% of the outcome and the remaining 70% is narrative framing, timing against the launch calendar, tiered amplification, first-hour choreography by the founder's real network, and a live war room. The most transferable lesson for meme-maker is that the frame you launch in ("agent-native meme engine" vs. generic "meme generator") sticks for a year, and that engineered controversy — a direct, debatable comparison — keeps the algorithm feeding the post long past its natural decay.

## Tactics

- **Start weeks out, not days (T-14).** A finished video is ~30% of a launch; hook, copy, timing, and amplification are the other 70%. `For meme-maker:` treat the Remotion video (PR #67) as one input and spend the remaining runway on copy, amplification lists, and timing — not more video polish.
- **Kill the wrong category label before launch (T-10).** "AI CMO" read as a threat to buyers, so they reframed to "autonomous AI marketer" — and it became the comment-thread shorthand by end of day. `For meme-maker:` pick the frame deliberately — e.g. "the meme engine for AI agents" / "give your agent a sense of humor" — and avoid "another meme generator," which frames it against ImgFlip instead of as agent infrastructure.
- **Dodge the crowded launch window (T-7).** Everyone launches Tuesday 7–9am PT; they moved to Monday 7am and beat every Tuesday launch that week. A less crowded feed beats a peak feed. `For meme-maker:` check what's launching that week (Product Hunt calendars, AI Twitter chatter) and pick an off-peak morning-PT slot rather than the default Tuesday.
- **Tier the amplifiers with distinct jobs (T-5).** ~30 top-tier voices doing independent QTs/threads, ~90 mid-tier ICP-adjacent accounts with substantive comments, ~180 momentum accounts on likes/reposts. `For meme-maker:` build a three-tier list from AI-agent/devtool Twitter — a handful of respected agent builders for QTs, mid-tier AI engineers for comments/demos, and a broad repost layer — each with tailored copy.
- **Engineer a debate (T-3, the "controversy" beat).** Briefing influencers to directly compare Helena against notable launches was risky but kept the thread alive on the algorithm for hours past normal decay. "Pick the right fight, publicly, and the algorithm rewards you." `For meme-maker:` seed a debatable angle — "should agents be allowed to shitpost?", meme-maker vs. LLM-generated images (deterministic templates vs. slop), or MCP-native tools vs. generic APIs.
- **The first hour belongs to people who actually know the founder (T-1).** Choreography: +00m founder posts, +05m investor/friend QTs, +15m substantive comments, +30m tier-1 influencers, +60m reach layers. The algorithm classifies the post on first-hour velocity; influencers come *after* the personal network. `For meme-maker:` put Kartik's real network on a calendar invite for the first 60–90 minutes with assigned time slots, before any broader amplification.
- **Run a live war room (T-0).** Dedicated channel, 90-minute call the night before, real-time replies all day. Founder doubt the night before is normal — the operation carries it. `For meme-maker:` block the full launch day; reply to every comment personally, casually, and fast — ideally replying *with memes generated by meme-maker itself*, which is a live demo in the mentions.
- **Judge by the KPI, not views (result).** Helena's win was 10x traffic and paying customers; Mave's was pre-orders. `For meme-maker:` define the metric up front — installs of the curl one-liner, GitHub stars, MCP server installs — and point every piece of copy at it (e.g. install command directly in the launch post or pinned reply).

## Don't do this

- Don't launch into the default Tuesday 7–9am PT pile-up alongside 5 other launches.
- Don't lead with influencers in hour one — velocity from strangers reads low-signal; personal network first.
- Don't accept the default category frame ("meme generator") — you'll live in the wrong comparison set for a year.
- Don't post-and-ghost: an unattended launch dies; no amount of later amplification revives a post that flopped its first hour.
- Don't reply in PR-speak; casual, funny, human replies only.
- Don't optimize for views as the goal — they're a proxy; track installs/stars.

## Next steps for the meme-maker launch

1. Lock the category frame and one-line positioning ("the meme engine for AI agents") and use it verbatim across video, post copy, README, and replies.
2. Map the week's launch calendar and pick an off-peak weekday 7am PT slot.
3. Build the 3-tier amplification list (agent-builder voices / AI-engineer mid-tier / repost layer) with per-tier custom copy.
4. Schedule the first-hour choreography with Kartik's personal network (calendar invite, assigned minute slots).
5. Prepare the debate angle (e.g. deterministic templates vs. AI image slop) and brief 2–3 accounts to run it ~30–60 min post-launch.
6. Set the launch-day KPI (installs via curl one-liner + GitHub stars) and stand up a war-room channel from the night before through T+24h, replying with live-generated memes.
