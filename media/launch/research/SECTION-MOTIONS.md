# Research: The Five Recent Marketing Motions (Subah Playbook)

Focus section: Monaco plane, Founders Fund MAFIA, HeyClickly raw founder video, Anthropic x Roberto Nickson, and the clipping examples (Lovable, Ramp, Replit, Wispr Flow, Emergent).

## Summary

The five motions share one trait: each cut through the noise by picking an unsaturated medium or an unusually authentic format instead of outspending the feed. Monaco won on pure novelty (a plane over SF where founders are billboard-blind), Founders Fund built owned entertainment people actually want to watch, HeyClickly proved a zero-budget raw founder demo with real energy can hit 3.6M impressions, Anthropic showed hyper-specific creator briefs beat generic influencer deals, and the clipping wave (Lovable, Replit, Wispr Flow, Emergent, Ramp) shows tech is finally adopting the streamer/musician playbook of flooding feeds with founder clips. For meme-maker — a free, open-source, agent-facing devtool — the raw founder video and novelty/self-demonstrating angles are the highest-leverage plays: the product literally makes shareable content, so the launch assets can be made *by* the product itself.

## Tactics

- **Find an unsaturated medium; novelty itself is the message (Monaco plane).** People went "wait, what is that?" — that confusion is attention, and it worked because it hadn't been done in SF before. `For meme-maker:` find the unsaturated angle on AI Twitter — e.g. an agent that replies to launch-thread comments with custom memes in real time, or a "meme-as-a-service" endpoint people can hit live; nobody has launched a devtool where the product roasts the launch thread itself.
- **Owned entertainment beats rented attention (Founders Fund MAFIA).** They adapted a timeless format (the party game Mafia) for a tech audience, filmed at a lore-rich location, and owned the resulting attention (1.5M views day one). `For meme-maker:` create a repeatable owned format, e.g. a recurring "AI agents make memes about this week in AI" series where different LLMs/agents compete — a show, not a one-off ad, that meme-maker powers each episode.
- **Raw founder energy beats production (HeyClickly / Farza).** Basically a Loom of himself demoing the product with huge energy, barely any copy, no funding-announcement hook — 3.6M impressions, 14k likes, 8.3k bookmarks with zero budget. `For meme-maker:` Kartik should record a raw screen-share demo — one take, high energy — of an agent generating memes live via CLI/MCP ("watch my agent shitpost for me"), and get straight into showing the thing in the first 3 seconds.
- **Show-don't-tell at the edge of capability (HeyClickly + Anthropic).** Both winning videos demo the product doing something at the edge of what people believe possible, inside a specific story. `For meme-maker:` the demo should show the *hardest* believable thing — an agent picking from 609 templates, auto-fitting text, rendering GIFs deterministically, and posting — not a slideshow of features.
- **Hyper-specialized creative briefs > generic influencer deals (Anthropic x Roberto Nickson).** A cinematic, absurd-but-specific day-in-the-life ("sorry babe, gotta go for training") did 1M+ views on IG and ~858k on X; specific briefs with the right creator outperform safe ones. `For meme-maker:` if partnering with an AI-Twitter creator, write one very specific brief (e.g. "your agent runs your meme account for 24 hours") rather than asking for a generic review.
- **The product can be its own distribution (clipping principle applied).** Clipping works because volume × variation wins the algorithm lottery: 100+ posts/week, most die, a few pop. `For meme-maker:` memes ARE the clip equivalent — generate dozens of launch-relevant memes (about MCP, agents, AI hype) with meme-maker itself and drip them out; every meme is a mini-ad demonstrating the product, watermark-light with the repo link in replies.
- **Repetition builds trust — the Rule of 7 (clipping section).** By the 7th time someone sees a founder/product in their feed, familiarity turns into trust (the Rogan effect); Lovable, Replit, and Wispr Flow all run daily founder-clip pages. `For meme-maker:` post meme-maker-generated content daily for weeks around launch, not one launch-day spike — sustained presence beats a single viral moment.
- **Personality and raw conviction are the fuel, not polish (clipping bottleneck).** "If your founder is media trained, clipping is not going to work" — the source content must be unfiltered, with strong takes and complete stories. `For meme-maker:` lean into unfiltered building-in-public takes (why agents need memes, hot takes on MCP, near-failure stories) rather than sanitized product copy.
- **Funnel attention back explicitly (clipping Step 7).** Bio links, small logo overlays, and tagging on every clip turned views into a 50% jump in branded search. `For meme-maker:` every posted meme/demo should carry the curl-installer one-liner or repo link in the first reply, and the GitHub link in bio; measure GitHub stars/installs, not impressions.

## Don't Do This (Anti-patterns)

- Don't over-produce: a polished agency-style launch video would undercut the indie/open-source authenticity that made HeyClickly work.
- Don't lead with vanity copy ("revolutionary", "game-changing") or a "we raised $X" hook — meme-maker has no raise; lead with the demo.
- Don't run a generic influencer deal ("please review my tool") — only hyper-specific creative briefs return.
- Don't post one launch tweet and hope: single posts without sustained follow-up volume die in the first hour.
- Don't copy a saturated format (yet another thread of "10 AI tools") — the whole section's lesson is unsaturated media wins.
- Don't be media-trained/corporate in replies; raw, funny, human responses keep threads alive.
