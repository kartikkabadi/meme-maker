# Launch Research: How the X Algorithm Works for Launches (Subah Playbook)

> Focus: the algorithm-mechanics tactics from Subah Wadhwani's playbook — video, influencers, verification/proof, copy, promo kits, comment section, hook, CTA, timing, tracking — applied to the meme-maker v0.3.1 X launch.
>
> Note: the source doc (`subah-playbook.md`) does not contain a literal "10 numbered steps" section; the 10 themes below are synthesized from the case studies (Mave, Helena, Composio, Lica, HeyClicky) and the clipping-campaign breakdown in that doc.

## Summary

The X algorithm classifies a launch post within its first 60 minutes based on engagement velocity; if the ratio is high, it pushes the post to a much larger audience, and no later amplification can save a post that dies in that window. The playbook's winning launches (Mave 2.5M, Helena 3.6M, Composio 1.9M) all pair a raw, show-don't-tell demo video with proof-led copy, a tiered and choreographed amplification plan (founder's real network first, influencers second), a comment section engineered for debate, and relentless real-time tracking from a war room. For meme-maker — a free, MIT, instantly-installable devtool aimed at AI Twitter — the playbook maps almost perfectly: the product IS memes, so the launch content can be self-demonstrating, and the CTA ("one curl command, try it now") is far lower friction than the $500 headset that made this playbook work.

## Tactics

- **Lead with a raw, energetic demo video, not a produced ad.** HeyClicky's launch was "basically a Loom recording" with a ton of energy — 3.6M impressions, 14k likes, zero budget. Authenticity beats production. `For meme-maker:` Kartik screen-records himself (or an agent) generating memes live — `curl` install to first rendered meme in under 60 seconds — with real energy, no polish; the Remotion video is secondary to a raw founder demo.
- **First hour = founder's real network, influencers after.** Helena's choreography: +00m founder posts, +05m investor circle QTs, +15m founder friends comment, +30m tier-1 influencer QTs, +60m tiers 2–3 layer in. The algorithm must already see the post as high-signal before influencers arrive. `For meme-maker:` set a calendar invite with friends/collaborators/past-PR contributors to engage in minutes 0–30, then ask AI-Twitter mutuals for QTs at +30–60m.
- **Tier your amplifiers and give each tier a different job.** Helena used ~30 top-tier voices (independent QTs/threads), ~90 mid-tier reach accounts, ~180 momentum accounts (likes/reposts), each verified for the target audience. `For meme-maker:` build a 3-tier list of AI-engineer/devtool accounts; tier 1 gets early access + a custom angle, tier 2 gets a promo kit, tier 3 just reposts on cue.
- **Proof in the first two lines of the copy — no hype words.** Mave opened with "We've raised $2.1M to fix your focus" and named Google/UFC/YC users; they cut "revolutionary" and "game-changing." `For meme-maker:` open with verifiable numbers — "609 templates, deterministic output, one curl command, MIT licensed" — not adjectives.
- **Ship promo kits with tiered copy per audience.** Mave wrote different angles for finance vs. health crowds so "the same product met each audience in the lingo they already cared about"; amplification means "writing a custom copy for each layer." `For meme-maker:` prepare 3 ready-to-post kits — agent-builder angle (MCP server for your agent), CLI/devtool angle (memes from your terminal), AI-Twitter angle (your agent can finally shitpost) — each with pre-rendered example memes.
- **Engineer the comment section for debate.** Helena's comparison play briefed influencers to compare it against notable launches; Composio launched into the MCP-vs-CLI holy war; "more debatable topic = more engagement" and debate keeps the post alive past the decay curve. Keep replies casual and funny, never PR speak. `For meme-maker:` seed a genuine debate — "should agents make memes?" / MCP vs CLI vs HTTP as the agent interface — and reply to everything with memes made by meme-maker itself.
- **Hook by showing the product at the edge of what it can do.** Anthropic's Nickson video worked because it was specific and cinematic, showing the product inside a memorable story; HeyClicky got straight into showing the thing. `For meme-maker:` the hook is an agent autonomously producing a perfect, timely meme with no human in the loop — show that in the first 3 seconds.
- **Make the CTA instant and conversion-oriented, and optimize one number.** Mave pointed every decision at US pre-orders, not views; the launch tweet ended "Order now at mavehealth.com." `For meme-maker:` pick one metric (GitHub stars or curl installs on launch day), and make the CTA a single copy-pasteable install command in the post itself.
- **Time the launch against the calendar, not with it, and stack press underneath.** Helena moved from the default Tuesday 7–9am PT slot to Monday 7am to dodge a crowded feed; Mave posted 2 hours after their TechCrunch story landed. `For meme-maker:` launch 7am PT on a low-competition day (check who else is launching that week); if any newsletter/HN/Product Hunt coverage exists, time the X post to land just after it.
- **Run a war room and track everything in real time.** Mave's team worked every reply and quote for hours from a dedicated Slack channel — including through a 40-minute X outage; winners are identifiable within 48 hours, then you double down on what's working. `For meme-maker:` block the full launch day; track impressions, installs, and stars hourly; when one angle/meme pops, immediately push follow-up posts and get amplifiers to QT that specific one.

## Don't do this

- Don't post and walk away — a launch is "won in real time"; unattended launches die in the first hour.
- Don't use hype words ("revolutionary," "game-changing") or PR-speak replies.
- Don't lead with influencers in the first 30 minutes — the algorithm needs organic-looking founder-network velocity first.
- Don't launch into a crowded Tuesday slot without checking who else is launching.
- Don't optimize for views — pick one conversion metric (installs/stars) and point every decision at it.
- Don't over-produce the video; a raw, high-energy screen recording outperforms polish.
- Don't pick a category frame carelessly — "the frame your launch lands in is the one you live with for the next 12 months."

## Next steps for the meme-maker launch

1. Lock the frame: one shorthand phrase (e.g. "the meme engine for AI agents") used consistently in video, copy, and briefs.
2. Cut a raw 45–60s founder demo: curl install → agent renders a meme, high energy, show-don't-tell.
3. Write the launch tweet: proof-led first two lines (609 templates, deterministic, MIT, one command) + install-command CTA.
4. Build the 3-tier amplifier list from AI/devtool Twitter and prepare per-audience promo kits with pre-rendered memes.
5. Schedule first-hour choreography (calendar invite for friends at +0–30m, tier-1 QTs at +30m) for 7am PT on a low-competition day.
6. Plan war-room day: reply to every comment with meme-maker-generated memes; track installs/stars hourly and double down on the winning angle within 48h.
